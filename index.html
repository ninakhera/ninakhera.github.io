Hey! I’m Nina Khera, a 13-year-old longevity & genomics researcher.

I’m working on a ton of sick projects right now. One to use gene sequencing to diagnose schizophrenia at an early stage, and another to find phenotypes in the mitochondria of senescent cells (Biotein - bioteinresearch.ca).

I’ve worked with sick companies like Wealthsimple and Walmart, spoken at Connect I.T., Collision, and will speak at Oakville Enterprises Innovation Hub.

In the future, I aspire to be a human longevity researcher working with senescent cells and impactful diseases, such as Epidermolysis Bullosa.

To stay up-to-date on what I’m doing, sign up for my monthly newsletter at http://eepurl.com/gcl0Vr.
